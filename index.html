Welcome

A will is a written document that sets out what you would like to happen to your possessions after you die. A will must be signed and witnessed. When you die, your possessions are called your ‘estate’. A ‘testator’ is a person who writes a will. If you die without leaving a will, you die ‘intestate’.

For crypto, you may die without leaving access to your keys. Here you can deposit Ether in a smart contract and enter beneficiaries. Then make periodic updates to indicate you are 'still alive'. If a certain interval has passed (illustrated here as 5 minutes, but may configurable) after the last 'Alive' message was received, the benefactor is presumed dead, and the funds may be distributed.

This is not a legal will.
